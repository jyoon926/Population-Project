How Sustainable Are You?

As we get nearer and nearer to reaching the Earth's human carrying capacity, and as the human population continues to grow at an unprecedented rate, the rate of our consumption of the planet's valuable resources is still increasing, while the planet struggles to regenerate.

In 2017, the global energy output was 14000 million tonnes of oil (Mtoe). This is the worldwide production of energy, extracted or captured directly from natural sources. In comparison, the average energy use per capita in the USA was 6.79762 tonnes of oil in 2015. If all 7.7 billion people on Earth were to live like the average American, we would need around 3.7 Earths in order to satisfy our current demands and desires.

6.79762 x 7.7 billion = 52341.674 million tonnes of oil

52341.674 million / 14000 million = 3.738691 Earths
