How Sustainable Are You?

We are on the verge of reaching the Earth's human carrying capacity, and as the human population continues to grow at an unprecedented rate, the rate of our consumption of the planet's valuable resources still increases uncontrollably, while the planet struggles to regenerate.

In 2017, the global energy output was 14,000 million tonnes of oil (Mtoe). This is the worldwide production of energy, extracted or captured directly from natural sources. In comparison, the average energy use per capita in a year in the USA was 6.79762 tonnes of oil in 2015. If all 7.7 billion people on Earth were to live like the average American, we would need around 3.7 Earths in order to satisfy our current demands and desires.

6.79762 x 7.7 billion = 52,341.674 Mtoe

52,341.674 million / 14,000 million = 3.738691 Earths

Another issue is water consumption. Fresh water is a limited and valuable resource on Earth, but our level of usage is extremely unsustainable. The withdrawal of freshwater per capita in the USA is around 1,600 cubic meters per year. Roughly 0.4% of the earth’s water is usable and drinkable, which exists in lakes and rivers with a volume of 93,113 cubic kilometers. If all 7.7 billion people on Earth were withdraw as much freshwater as the average American, we would need around 130,000 Earths.

1,600 m3 x 7.7 billion = 12,320 million km3

12,320 million / 93,113 = 132,312 Earths

x 130,000

It is now more important than ever to change our ways and make better choices on how we use our planet's limited resources. Population will continue to increase, but the Earth will not be able to adapt.
